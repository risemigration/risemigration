UNDERSTANDING THE
GSM POINTS SYSTEM

Age Thresholds

The migration system prioritizes younger applicants who contribute longer to the tax base. The maximum points (30) are awarded between ages 25 and 32. At age 45, eligibility for General Skilled Migration typically ceases.

English Proficiency

English is a major score multiplier. While "Competent" (IELTS 6) grants 0 points, "Superior" English (IELTS 8/PTE 79) grants a massive 20-point boost, often being the difference between a grant and a refusal.

Skilled Employment

Experience must be "post-qualification" to be counted for points. Overseas experience is capped at 15 points (8+ years), while Australian experience is valued more highly, offering up to 20 points.

Bonus Points (Boosters)

STEM Qualifications (+10 Points)

Master by Research or PhD in Science, Technology, Engineering, or Mathematics from an Australian institution.

NAATI CCL (+5 Points)

Accreditation in a community language through NAATI testing.

Regional Study (+5 Points)

Completing a degree while living and studying in a designated regional area of Australia.

State Nomination (190/491)

Securing a State Nomination adds points to your profile automatically:

  • • Subclass 190 Nomination: +5 Points (PR)
  • • Subclass 491 Nomination: +15 Points (Provisional)
